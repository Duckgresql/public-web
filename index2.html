When Postgres does everything, nothing runs well

Transactional and analytical workloads have opposite requirements. Forcing both into one database creates predictable pain.

  • 1
    Lock contention and slow writes Heavy analytics queries hold locks and block your OLTP. Writes stall; users wait.
  • 2
    Scaling costs explode You scale the whole instance for analytics spikes. You pay for the wrong kind of scale.
  • 3
    ETL pipelines and duplication You sync data out to a warehouse or another DB. More moving parts, more latency, more ops.
  • 4
    Latency you can't fix with indexes OLAP and OLTP compete for the same resources. One always loses‚Äîusually both.
